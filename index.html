Peter Gonda (aka gnd, 1983) is a theatre director, vj and computer scientist.

In 2004 gnd became a member of Bratislava's first media lab Burundi, and after its demise co-founded the Itchy Bit collective, focused on happenings on the intersection of art, social issues and technology. During his studies of Applied Computer Sciences and Cognitive Sciences at Comenius University in Bratislava he became infatued with the mother of Slovak underground radios - the student internet radio Tlis.sk, where he later became the editor-in-chief.

Situated on the intersection of computing technology and arts, in 2008 he became the administrator of the free arts server Sanchez, as well as helped to design, develop and bootstrap the streaming archive of The Slovak Philharmonic Orchestra. In 2010, after leaving radio Tlis, he co-founded Easterndaze.net, an ongoing project dedicated to exploring underground music scenes of CEE countries, and later in 2013 co-started it's sister tape label Baba Vanga, that releases experimental electronica. His passion for radio and music kept him in touch with Czech Radio channel 4 - Radio Wave where he currently works as an external contributing editor.

Apart from performing solo, gnd is also a member of Kolektiv - a loose improvisational / live-coding body of creators of algorithmic music and visuals based in Prague. His visuals are usually live-coded in environments such as Pure Data, his own glslr, or pijockey on Raspberry PI.

Besides his visual work, gnd holds a MA from theatre direction at the DAMU Theatre Academy in Prague. Since 2010 he directed several theatre performances often working with non-actors or mixed ensembles of professional actors and non-actors.

Young people take the stage and address their elders, who have always told them what they can and cannot do. The kids are taught about freedom provided they remain quiet. This time, the adults will stay silent, „because when children speak, adults listen.“

Lil Drama is a theatre project with teenagers directed by Peter Gonda. Since October 2024, Gonda and his team have worked with a group of young participants to explore themes of oppression, violence, and resistance. The result is not a play for children, but a performance by children for adults. Lil Drama is a series of experiments - an effort to understand the world, reflect on it, change it, and, when necessary, negate it.

Finger guns can kill. AI can suffer. Puppets are murdered. Stage-slap punches become playful yet terrifying tools of escalating violence. "Capitalism is that kind of slime they sell in a glittering pink tube marked „limited edition“, but when you open it, it reeks like chemicals and scorches your tabletop." Mladí si berou slovo a obracejí se ke starším, kteří jim říkají co smí a co nesmí. Učí je také o svobodě – ale jen když jsou zticha! Tentokrát budou mlčet dospělí. „Protože, když mluví děti, dospělí poslouchají.“

Lil Drama je divadelní projekt s dospívajícími lidmi v režii Petra Gondy. Gonda a jeho kolektiv se od října 2024 setkávali se skupinou mladého účastnictva, aby společně zkoumali téma útlaku, násilí a vzdoru. Vzniklo tak představení nikoli pro děti, ale představení dětí pro dospělé. Lil Drama je sérií pokusů: pokus o pochopení světa, o jeho reflexi, o změnu, i o jeho negaci.

Finger guns mohou zabíjet. AI může trpět. Loutky se navzájem mordují. Fake punches se stávají nástroji hravé, ale děsivé eskalace násilí. "Kapitalizmus je přesně ten druh slizu, který prodávají v růžové třpytivé tubě s nápisem „limited edition“, ale když ji otevřeš, je cítit po chemikáliích a rozleptá ti stůl.“

Is there any wilderness left? This audiovisual production by Peter Gonda blurries the line between "pristine" nature and what humanity projects into it.

The Moravian Karst is a protected natural reserve in the proximity of Czechia's second city - Brno. It is a region with a long history of human activity: evidence was found of Neanderthals living in the Kůlna cave 120,000 years ago, and a settlement, today known as Brno, gradually grew near Stránská skála (the Stránská Rock) in a place where traces of human activity more then 600 00 years BPE were found. Rich in minerals, the area was later a hotspot for mining activities with a rich industrial tradition while the woods became a projection screen for urban dreams about nature that invite us to go for a relaxing stroll. Images from a past long gone and near future. What geological layer will human activity leave behind? Existuje ještě vůbec divočina? Autorská audiovizuální inscenace Petera Gondy o nejasné hranici mezi netknutou přírodou a tím, co si do ní lidstvo projektuje.

Moravský kras je uzákoněný jako chráněná krajinná oblast, ohraničené, vymezené a korigované území. Je také záznamníkem tisíců let lidské činnosti: v jeskyni Kůlna byl doložen pobyt člověka neandrtálského z doby před 120 000 lety, poblíž Stránské skály se postupně rozrůstalo osídlení dnes známé jako Brno. Nerostné bohatství poznamenala těžba a průmyslová tradice, z lesů se naopak stalo projekční plátno městských snů o přírodě, do které lze zavítat na oddechovou návštěvu. Obrazy z dávné minulosti i blízké budoucnosti. Jaká je geologická vrstva, kterou po sobě zanechává lidské působení?
